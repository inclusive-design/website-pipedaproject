Andrea Slane
Tuesday, 25 January 2005
Andrea Slane B.A. (High Honours) (Rutgers) 1986, PhD (Comparative Literature) (UCSD) 1995, J.D. (Honours) (Toronto) 2003, is a visiting fellow at the Centre for Innovation Law and Policy. Her current projects examines legal approaches to unsolicited bulk email (spam) and child luring on the Internet, with regard to how implemented and proposed solutions adhere to varying conceptions of democracy.  
 

While at Olser, Hoskin __ Harcourt LLP she drafted submissions to the Minister of Health and Long-Term Care on Ontario's proposed health privacy legislation for the Roundtable on Health Informatics and Privacy, a group consisting of government and private industry members in both health and network technology sectors.  Her book, A Not So Foreign Affair: Fascism, Sexuality and the Cultural Rhetoric of American Democracy, was published by Duke University Press in 2001.
